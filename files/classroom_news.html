Teddy Bears (2.5-3 year olds)

Science and Nature:

Observed and interacted with baby chicks, learning about their behavior.

Studied the lifecycle of caterpillars turning into butterflies.

Participated in gardening activities, planting and caring for the garden.

Autumn exploration with activities like playing with, painting, and measuring leaves.

Observing changes in the season, including snow on leaves.

Art:

Activities around color theory, including creating a color wheel and mixing paints.

Sensory exploration with colored water.

Literacy:

Reading color-themed books like "Bear Sees Colors" and "Pete the Cat: I Love My White Shoes."

Social Skills:

Learning manners during snack time, including saying "Please" and "Thank You," and cleaning up.

Math:

Counting, matching, and comparing leaf sizes.

Physical Development:

Enhancing fine motor skills through art activities like painting leaves.

Gross motor skill activities like raking and jumping in leaves.


Panda Bears (2.5-3 year olds)

Science: Explored hibernation with real animals and a thematic song.

Culinary Arts: Cooked buttermilk pancakes and baked holiday breads in pajamas.

Art and Craft: Created holiday-themed artwork including collages and painted decorations.

Cultural Studies: Studied Hanukkah traditions and shared gelt.


Bear Cubs (3-4 year olds)

Science and Nature:

Observed and interacted with baby chicks, enhancing understanding of animal behavior.

Monitored the transformation of caterpillars into butterflies, exploring insect lifecycles.

Engaged in gardening, learning about plant growth and the importance of care in the natural world.

Social and Community Studies:

Celebrated "Muffins with Mom," fostering community connections and family engagement within the classroom.


Big Bears (4-5 year olds)

Science and Nature:

Planted tulips and violets, teaching about plant growth and care.

Upcoming chick unit to chart and observe chick growth, introducing animal development and care.

Civics and Social Studies:

Mock voting activity to introduce concepts of democracy and fairness.

Art and Creativity:

Watercolor painting and weaving with yarn to foster artistic skills.

Created a zoo with stuffed animals and blocks, encouraging imaginative play.

Math and Analysis:

Graphed "yam fries" taste test results, teaching data collection and analysis.

Literacy and Language:

Completed ABC books to reinforce alphabet knowledge.

Celebrated the end of the ABC unit with alphabet-themed foods.

Culinary Arts:

Conducted a "yam fries" taste test, integrating sensory experiences with learning.


Sun Bears (4-5 year olds)

Time Concept:

Activities around understanding time, using clock faces, and reading "Clocks and More Clocks."

Physical Education:

Baseball-related activities including shape exploration on a baseball field, crafting with laminated gloves and painting bats and balls, and enjoying baseball-themed books.

Science and Nature:

Observing lifecycle changes: chicks hatching, caterpillars to chrysalises, and garden blooming.

Literacy:

Reading and engaging with baseball-themed literature such as "Curious George Plays Ball," "Amelia Bedelia Plays Ball," and "Froggy Plays T-Ball."

Art and Craft:

Creative projects related to time and baseball, including making clock faces and decorating baseball equipment.
